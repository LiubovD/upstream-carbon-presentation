= Mapping Favorable Chanterelle Habitat in Rhode Island

Mapping Favorable Chanterelle Habitat

Using soils and forest data to identify high-probability locations in Rhode Island

Problem

Chanterelles depend on specific soil and forest conditions, but most foraging is trial and error. I wanted to use GIS data to identify favorable environments before going into the field.

Datasets

Soils 2025
Detailed soil map units from the Rhode Island Soil Survey Program.
Ecological Communities (2011)
Statewide forest and habitat classification derived from imagery and LiDAR.

Approach

  • Lippitt gravelly sandy loam (LgC) – well-drained upland soils
  • Canton & Charlton very stony fine sandy loams (ChC) – classic upland forest soils
  • Canton–Charlton–Rock outcrop complex (CaD) – rocky ridge habitat
  • Hinckley gravelly sandy loam (HkC) – sandy, well-drained soils
  • Sutton extremely stony fine sandy loam (SvB) – coarse upland soils

Combined with:

  • Oak Forest
  • Hemlock/Hardwood Forest
  • Mixed Deciduous/Coniferous Forests

Solution

I intersected soil polygons with forest habitat polygons to isolate areas where both conditions align. The result is a GeoJSON layer of favorable chanterelle habitat.

Action

I published the dataset to GitHub and built this interactive map to guide real-world scouting.
