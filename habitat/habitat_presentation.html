🌳 Mapping Favorable Chanterelle Habitat in Rhode Island

Using soils and forest data to identify where chanterelles are most likely to grow.

Problem & Motivation

Chanterelles grow in specific ecological conditions, but most foraging relies on trial and error. I wanted to use public environmental data to predict where favorable conditions exist before going into the field.

This map shows a lightweight sample of the full dataset for performance.

Datasets

Soils 2025
Detailed soil map unit polygons from the Rhode Island Soil Survey Program (URI EDC + RIGIS).
Ecological Communities Classification (2011)
Statewide habitat classification derived from imagery, LiDAR, and land cover data.

Approach

  • Selected favorable upland soils based on drainage and texture
  • Selected forest habitats with likely mycorrhizal host trees
  • Intersected soil and habitat layers
  • Exported result as GeoJSON

Prime Habitat Types

🌳 Oak Forest

Best soils:

  • Canton & Charlton very stony fine sandy loams (ChB / ChC / ChD)
  • Canton–Charlton–Rock outcrop complex (CaC / CaD)
  • Hinckley gravelly sandy loam (HkC / HkD)
  • Lippitt gravelly sandy loam (LgC)
  • Sutton extremely stony fine sandy loam (SuB)

Highest-probability: ChC + Oak Forest

🌲 Hemlock / Hardwood Forest

Best soils:

  • Hinckley soils (HkA / HkC / HkD)
  • Canton & Charlton soils (ChC)
  • Sutton soils (SvB)
  • Lippitt soils (LgC)

Highest-probability: HkC + Hemlock/Hardwood

🌿 Mixed Forest

Best soils:

  • Hinckley gravelly sandy loam (HkC)
  • Canton & Charlton soils (ChC)
  • Canton–Charlton–Rock outcrop complex (CaD)
  • Lippitt soils (LgC)
  • Woodbridge soils (WoB)

🍁 Northern Hardwood Forest

Best soils:

  • Canton & Charlton soils (ChC)
  • Canton–Charlton–Rock outcrop complex (CaD)
  • Sutton soils (SvB)
  • Charlton extremely stony soils (CeC)

Solution

The result is a spatial layer highlighting areas where soil and forest conditions align. Instead of searching randomly, this map identifies high-value areas to prioritize in the field.
